Origin Note

The name Redwire comes from an old saying in the undercity. No verified source has claimed ownership of the phrase, and most witnesses refuse to repeat it twice.

“Cut the red wire, and everything goes dark.”

Interpretations vary. Some say it refers to bomb circuitry. Others say communication lines. A few older sources claim it was once tied to a founding bloodline, though those records have been removed from public review.

Primary Economy

In Meridian Spire, information is currency. Redwire does not simply sell secrets. They purchase, steal, barter, bury, and weaponize them.

  • Corporate blackmail material
  • Government records
  • Personal leverage files
  • Surveillance intercepts
  • Financial laundering trails

Known Calling Cards

Scenes linked to Redwire activity often include one or more repeated markers:

  • Red wire left on a victim’s wrist
  • Envelopes wrapped in red thread
  • Radios emitting static before arrival

BCI Threat Assessment

Redwire’s danger does not come from numbers alone. It comes from leverage. A Redwire threat can look like a missed payment, a police visit, a broken marriage, or a burning storefront.

TRACE RETURNED: signal bounced through 14 undercity relays. Origin unresolved. Final packet contained only static and a single line:

DO NOT PULL THE WIRE.

Protection Operations

Businesses under Redwire “protection” receive services that resemble law enforcement, private security, and organized extortion depending on who is asked.

  • SecurityVisible deterrence, armed presence, and quiet removal of threats.
  • Debt MediationDisputes settled fast. Terms are rarely negotiable twice.
  • ResolutionLocal arguments, vendor conflicts, and gang pressure handled off-record.

Refusal Pattern

Individuals and businesses who refuse Redwire terms are commonly linked to one or more retaliatory events.

  • FinancialFrozen accounts, lost invoices, fabricated debts, missing deposits.
  • PropertyArson, utility failure, broken locks, staged break-ins.
  • LegalFalse criminal accusations supported by planted records.
  • FamilyThreats delivered indirectly, usually with proof of surveillance.

Red Market

Lower-street couriers move sealed envelopes, broken radios, and drive shards through market crowds. Most runners do not know what they carry.

Wire Rooms

Temporary listening posts built inside abandoned service closets. They appear for a night, harvest a block, then vanish before dawn.

Quiet Courts

Backroom dispute hearings where debts, apologies, and disappearances are negotiated by people who never give their real names.

BCI analysts believe Redwire is not a traditional gang. It behaves more like a decentralized archive with weapons. Leadership may not be a single person, but a chain of handlers who each control one section of the network.

Confirmed hierarchy remains withheld. Field agents are advised not to ask who leads Redwire unless they already know what Redwire has on them.
